Meditation Space The site situated very rural area at Buttala, Badalkumbura on the top of the mountain which is covered with thick forest. Initial client (donor) requirement was to demolish existing Kutiya which was notwithstanding the site conditions and rebuild new one. In terms of architectural designing we identified some issues and tried to provide creative and effective solutions.
Location Buttala, Badalkumbura, Sri Lanka
Involvement Architectural design and development as a principal architect
Client Mahamevnawa Buddhist Monastery, Sri Lanka
Status Completed in January 2023
Solution / Concept Less impact to the nature and with stand for the unforeseeable weather conditions
Minimalizing the damage to the sensitive forest environment In the planning stage we visited the site location and identified the site condition, kind of building type and foundation type to solve above problems. With my team, we decided to go for the Individual Footings system with lifted ground floor like traditional “Tampita vihara” concept. Therefor, we planned only 4 column structure which is distributing the load of the structure to the ground and low building footprint. From that concept we have minimalized the damage to the forest environment and also it gives proper protection to monks from the wildlife. Therefor you can feel the environment within the safe zone.
Material transportation difficulties Since this location is at the top of the hill and into the jungle We had to take materials by hand for about 2 Km from the access road. Material transportation was a huge task for this kind of location and we cannot use heavy machineries due to the forest environment and also it disturbs the other monk who meditates surround environment. The most reliable method was an overhead conveyer cable with small electric motor and additionally human power. Since this is a religious activity most of the villagers provided their support free of charge.
Reduce the construction cost To achieve this we tried to introduce reliable regional materials like regional wood for windows, doors and roof structure, River sand collected from the village river from villagers. Alternative materials for floor and roof.
